From a preliminary check, it seems ORB is more reliable for key point tracking in this application based off the test imagery of checkered boards and small background objects which will be relevant in the experiments used for the VO pipeline (camera being moved around my room or library with checkered pattern in frame at the start or end and small background objects). This is qualitatively evident in ORB_test1 versus SIFT_test1 when comparing how many keypoints were assigned to the checkerboard versus a fairly small background object of a bottle cap and how many points were accurately tracked across orientation. SIFT_test1 assigns way more key points around the stationary bottle cap (instead of distributing them across objects more evenly) and way less to what would be a desirable to be a main focus, the checkered pattern being moved, while still fairly inaccurately tracking th points it did assign to it. For this reason, ORB will be used going forward.

K-cluster and DBscan were tested in tandem with RANSAC for filtering important keypoints. K-clusters has an unclear tuning process, with certain cluster counts doing better than others, but not in an increasing fashion but a random alternating pattern. In general, DBscan seems to be more efficient since kclusters demands a higher count around 12 at least and with ease selects matches that are more accurate (at worst 2 squares off with most 1 or less) while k-clusters typically is 4-5 squares off. This is likely thanks to the epsilon parameter which allows DBscan to eliminate clusters with points too far from one another, while kclusters compacts points together based on how many clusters are expected to be taken from the total. Therefore, with kclusters it's less straight forward and computationally expensive (b/c more clusters are required) to compact the points together and filter out squares with inaccurately spread out matches). For now, DBscan will be moved forward.
